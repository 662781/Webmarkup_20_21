Solar Panels

Solar panels are constructed of a layer of silicon cells, a metal frame, a glass casing surrounded by a special foil and a lot of wires. The solar-cells, which are also called photovoltaic cells, absorb the sunlight.

Charge Controller

The charge controller makes sure that the battries in the system do not get overcharged. It regulates the current and voltage coming from the solar panels going into the battries. Most 12 volt solar panels have an output of more than 16 volts. So if there was nothing to control the current and voltage, the batteries would be permanently damaged.

Battery System

If your solar panels produce more energy than necessary, the power is loaded into the battery system. Most battery systems have their own inverter and charge controller. The battery system lets you store a lot of power for later use. It makes your solar panel project efficient and sustainable!

DC Power

If everything works correctly, you'll have solar panels working efficiently to transform sunlight into electricity. The electricity generated is called direct current, also called "DC", electricity

AC power

AC or "alternating current" electricity is used by most homes to power their devices. First you need to convert the DC electricity. That is the job of the so called "Inverter".
